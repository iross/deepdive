3.2. Description

The fossils presented here are seed—like reproductive organs. They might be bilaterally symmetrical before compression. Overall contours are ovate, obovate or tubular, 7.3—10 mm long and 3.5-4.1 mm wide (Fig. 3). The base is slightly oblique, cuneate or attenuate into a curved stalk (Fig. 3E, F). The apex opens ﬁmbriately. The outermost part of the reproductive struc- ture, when being open, forms an inverted “S—shaped” lateral appendage (Fig. 3B, C), which is interpreted as the sporophyll. The surface of the sporophyll bears parallel striations and appears to form an acute angle with the longitudinal axis of a seed—like organ (Fig. 3E, F). The base of the sporophyll is connected with an ovate or tubular central body, which is interpreted as the sporangium. Hence, this seed—like organ consists of a sporangium—sporophyll complex; sometimes, the sporangium is detached from the sporophyll (Fig. 3A, D). The base of the sporangium is cuneate. The apex of the sporangium is ﬁmbriately open. The surface of the sporangium possesses striations along greater diameter. The sporangium appears to contain one larger (presumably functional) megaspore and much smaller (presumably abortive) megaspores, which can be recognized by different colors of coaliﬁcation (Fig. 3B, C). The spatial arrangement of the functional megaspore and terminal, abortive megaspores appears to be linear (Fig. 3C). The functional megaspore is roughly elliptical, about 4.3 mm long and 2.7 mm wide. The parent plant is unknown.

3.3. Identity

The fertile organs presented here are very similar to a seed—like structure, i.e., Sphinxiocarpon wahaniam (Li, Hilton et Hemsley) Wang, Xue et Prestianni (=Sphinxia wahania Li, Hilton et Hemsley), which was ﬁrst reported from the coeval sediments of Miliangshan Quarry of Wuhan, eastern Hubei (Li et al., 1997) and then from Changyang, western Hubei (Xue, 2010). Miliangshan and Xianniishan are two adjacent quarries (Fig. 1B). The primary difference among the specimens from all these three localities is that the seed—like organ collected from Xianniishan Quarry possesses a ﬁmbriately opened apex and clearly shows the attaching relationship of its sporophyll and sporangium. However, we do not think that the ﬁmbriately opened apex represents an innate dehiscence structure of this seed—like organ. Instead, the ﬁmbriately opened apex of its spo- rangium and sporophyll is far more likely a result of the tissular dissolution and mechanical dynamics within the sporophyll and sporangial wall when the seed—like organ is mature in the ﬁnal developmental stage. Hence, we classify these seed—like organs from Xianniishan Quarry into S. wahaniam.

3.4. Comparisons

Sphinxiocarpon wahaniam is noticeably different from the radially or bilaterally symmetrical preovules and seeds of Mid—Late Devonian to Early Carboniferous, because the lat- ter have speciﬁcally modiﬁed apexes (i.e., distal extension or pollen chamber) of the megasporangium and innate dehis- cence structures of the integument, e.g., Rancaria heinzelinii
